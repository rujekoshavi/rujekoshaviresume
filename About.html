About Me

Hello! I’m Rujeko Shavi, a Master’s student in Software Engineering at Kennesaw State University. With a strong foundation in Biology and several years of professional laboratory experience, I’m passionate about combining science and technology to create innovative solutions in the biotech space.

Background

I hold a Bachelor of Science degree in Biology from Northern State University. During my undergraduate and professional career, I gained hands-on research and lab experience, including RNA/DNA extraction, PCR, molecular biology techniques, and quality control in clinical lab environments. I have worked with instruments such as Alinity M, Integra Viaflo 96, and performed detailed lab procedures to ensure accuracy and reliability of results.

Why Software Engineering?

Working in labs gave me first-hand exposure to the critical role of technology in research and healthcare. I became fascinated by the potential of software to improve efficiency, automate workflows, and unlock new possibilities in biotech and life sciences. This inspired me to pursue a graduate degree in Software Engineering, where I am learning programming, system design, data analysis, and software development practices to complement my science background.

Future Goals

My long-term goal is to work at the intersection of biotechnology and software engineering. I aim to design and build tools that support researchers, healthcare professionals, and pharmaceutical companies in solving complex biological problems. By merging biology and technology, I hope to contribute to innovations that improve healthcare outcomes and advance scientific discovery.

Beyond Work

Outside of academics and professional projects, I enjoy exploring new technologies, attending hackathons, and collaborating on group projects. I am also passionate about supporting women in STEM and fostering mentorship opportunities for students entering science and technology fields.
